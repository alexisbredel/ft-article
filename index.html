BMO Fined Amid Scandal, Closing New York Office
Bank of Montreal to Relocate Employees to Houston and Edmonton to Support Fossil Fuel Expansion

The Bank of Montreal (BMO) is facing severe regulatory penalties following an extensive investigation into its U.S. operations. As a result, the Canadian banking giant has announced it will be shutting down its New York City office, with plans to relocate affected employees to Houston and Edmonton.

Regulators cited "serious compliance failures" that led to a record fine, mirroring the recent scandal involving TD Bank. Sources indicate that financial misreporting and irregularities in cross-border transactions triggered the investigation.

"This restructuring is a strategic decision to focus on emerging market opportunities," said a BMO spokesperson. "With rising global energy demands, reallocating resources to key energy hubs like Houston and Edmonton aligns with our long-term objectives."

Industry analysts suggest the move could solidify BMO’s role in financing the fossil fuel sector, a move likely to draw criticism from environmental groups. Meanwhile, employees in New York are scrambling to weigh their relocation options.
